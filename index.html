How much fast food is there really in America?

by Giselle Medina | November 19, 2021

Americans love a good fast food restaurant. You can’t walk the streets of New York City without seeing a McDonalds or a Taco Bell every couple of blocks. It makes one wonder, how many fast food restaurants are there really in America?

In 2018, Datafiniti conducted a study to gather all the fast food restaurants in America. They extracted data of the locations and then sorted them geographically to visualize which areas in the country had the highest concentration.

An analysis of data from Datafiniti reveals that Kentucky, at 7.57 fast food restaurants per 10K residents, has the highest number of fast food restaurants per capita.

New York, at 1.28 fast food restaurants per 10K residents, has the least number of fast food restaurants per capita.

It’s apparent that people and their likes vary from state to state with some restaurants being more popular than others. So, what are the most popular fast food joints?

Based off this map, the more concentrated areas are in the Central region of the country.

Delving deeper into Datafiniti’s data, fast food chains such as Subway and McDonald’s were almost always in a state’s top three fast food restaurants.

If there’s one thing about America, it’s that fast food restaurants are not scarce and very apparent in every state.

by Giselle Medina for Fundamentals of Data Journalism, Fall 2021
